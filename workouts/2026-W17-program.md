# Week B — Full Body: Squat + Pull + Glutes
**Week of**: April 20, 2026 | **Cycle**: B of 4 | **Est. Time**: 40–45 min
**Focus**: Quads · Lats · Glutes · Rear Delts · Core

> Every session works your whole body — lower, pull, and core together.
> Rest 45–60 seconds between sets to keep your heart rate up for lean muscle.

---

## Block 1 — Strength (25–28 min)

### 1. Goblet Squat `3 × 15`
- **Target muscles**: Quads, glutes, core
- **Weight**: 50–60 lb KB/DB
- Wide stance, toes out. Chest tall throughout.

### 2. Lat Pulldown (Cable) `3 × 12`
- **Target muscles**: Lats, biceps, rear delts
- **Weight**: 80–100 lbs
- Lean back slightly. Pull bar to upper chest. Squeeze lats at the bottom.

### 3. Hip Thrust (Glute Bridge) `3 × 15`
- **Target muscles**: Glutes, hamstrings
- **Weight**: 45–70 lb DB across hips
- Upper back on bench. Full hip extension. Hold 1 second at top.

### 4. Face Pull (Cable) `3 × 15`
- **Target muscles**: Rear delts, rotator cuff, upper traps
- **Weight**: 30–40 lbs
- Cable at face height. Pull to forehead, elbows high and wide. Essential for shoulder health.

### 5. Bulgarian Split Squat `3 × 10 each leg`
- **Target muscles**: Quads, glutes, hip flexors
- **Weight**: 30–40 lbs total (2 DBs)
- Rear foot on bench. Control the descent. Great for single-leg stability.

---

## Block 2 — Core (12–15 min)

### 6. Ab Rollout (from knees) `3 × 10`
- Keep lower back from sagging. Extend as far as stable.

### 7. Side Plank `3 × 35 sec each side`
- Full body in one line. Option to add hip dip for extra work.

### 8. Cable Woodchop (High to Low) `3 × 12 each side`
- **Weight**: 30–40 lbs
- Rotate from torso, not just arms. Control the return.

---

## Notes
- Face pulls counteract all pressing — this is how you keep shoulders healthy long-term.
- Lat pulldown balances the pushing from Week A's bench press and shoulder press.
- Bulgarian split squats build single-leg stability — great carryover to hiking and bouldering.
- Keep rest short (45–60 sec) for lean muscle; this is functional strength, not powerlifting.
