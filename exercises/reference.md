# Exercise Reference Library

> **Training Philosophy**: Toning + lean muscle + fat loss. Moderate weight, higher reps (12–15), minimal rest (60–90s). Prioritize form over load.
> Both athletes do yoga daily and boulder weekly — core stability and mobility are already strong foundations.

---

## PUSH — Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

### Dumbbell Bench Press
- **Muscles**: Chest (primary), front delts, triceps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Neutral spine, feet flat. Control the eccentric (3s down). Clint: start ~70–80 lbs total. Wife: start ~20–30 lbs total.
- **Bouldering Connection**: Antagonist push movement — balances the pulling/crimping in climbing.

### Incline Dumbbell Press
- **Muscles**: Upper chest, front delts
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: 30–45° incline. Don't flare elbows excessively.

### Dumbbell Shoulder Press
- **Muscles**: Deltoids (all heads), triceps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Can be seated or standing. Standing engages more core.

### Lateral Raises
- **Muscles**: Medial deltoids
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15
- **Notes**: Light weight only. Slight forward lean, lead with elbows. Clint: 15–25 lbs. Wife: 5–10 lbs.

### Tricep Dips (Bench)
- **Muscles**: Triceps, front delts
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15
- **Notes**: Keep hips close to bench. Legs extended for more challenge.

### Tricep Pushdown (Cable)
- **Muscles**: Triceps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15
- **Notes**: Keep elbows pinned at sides. Full extension at bottom.

### Push-Ups (Weighted or Bodyweight)
- **Muscles**: Chest, triceps, core
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15–20
- **Notes**: Can add weight plate on back. Great finisher.

## PULL — Back, Biceps, Rear Delts

### Lat Pulldown
- **Muscles**: Lats, biceps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Full stretch at top. Pull to upper chest. Wide or neutral grip variants.

### Seated Cable Row
- **Muscles**: Mid back, lats, biceps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Sit tall. Squeeze shoulder blades. Don't lean back excessively.

### Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
- **Muscles**: Lats, mid back, biceps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15 each side
- **Notes**: Support on bench. Keep back flat. Full range of motion.

### Face Pulls (Cable)
- **Muscles**: Rear delts, rotator cuff, upper traps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15–20
- **Notes**: Use rope attachment at face height. Pull to ears. Great for shoulder health and bouldering.

### Dumbbell Bicep Curl
- **Muscles**: Biceps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Full supination at top. Don't swing. Alternating or simultaneous.

### Hammer Curl
- **Muscles**: Brachialis, biceps, forearms
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Neutral grip throughout. Excellent for climber forearm strength.

### Assisted Pull-Ups / Chin-Ups
- **Muscles**: Lats, biceps, core
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 8–12
- **Notes**: Use assisted machine or band if needed. Wife: likely needs assistance initially. Directly transfers to bouldering.

## LEGS — Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes, Calves

### Goblet Squat
- **Muscles**: Quads, glutes, core
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15
- **Notes**: Wide stance, toes out slightly. Hold dumbbell/kettlebell at chest. Great for both athletes.

### Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
- **Muscles**: Hamstrings, glutes, lower back
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Hinge at hips, soft knee bend. Feel the hamstring stretch. Keep bar/dumbbells close to legs.

### Bulgarian Split Squat
- **Muscles**: Quads, glutes, hip flexors
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 10–12 each leg
- **Notes**: Rear foot on bench. Great single-leg stability for bouldering footwork.

### Leg Press
- **Muscles**: Quads, glutes, hamstrings
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15
- **Notes**: Don't lock knees at top. Foot position varies muscle emphasis.

### Walking Lunges
- **Muscles**: Quads, glutes, balance
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12 each leg
- **Notes**: Dumbbells in each hand. Step length affects glute vs quad emphasis.

### Glute Bridge / Hip Thrust
- **Muscles**: Glutes, hamstrings
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15
- **Notes**: Add barbell or dumbbell across hips for load. Full hip extension at top.

### Sumo Deadlift
- **Muscles**: Inner thighs, glutes, hamstrings
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 10–12
- **Notes**: Wide stance, toes out. Good hip mobility required (yoga helps here).

### Calf Raises (Standing)
- **Muscles**: Calves
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 20–25
- **Notes**: Full range — all the way up and all the way down. Slow tempo.

## FULL BODY COMPOUND

### Kettlebell Swing
- **Muscles**: Posterior chain, core, cardio
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 20
- **Notes**: Power from hips, not arms. Hinge pattern. Heart rate elevating. Clint: 53 lbs. Wife: 26–35 lbs.

### Dumbbell Thruster
- **Muscles**: Full body — squat to press
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12
- **Notes**: Efficient total-body movement. Great for fat burning.

### Dumbbell Deadlift
- **Muscles**: Full posterior chain
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12
- **Notes**: Good entry point to deadlift pattern. Hip hinge focus.

### Renegade Row
- **Muscles**: Back, core, arms in plank position
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 10 each side
- **Notes**: Start lighter than expected — anti-rotation core is demanding.

### TRX Row (or Ring Row)
- **Muscles**: Back, biceps, core
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Adjust body angle to vary difficulty. Very bouldering-friendly.

## CORE — The Priority

> Core is included in EVERY session. The goal: functional stability, not just aesthetics.

### Dead Bug
- **Muscles**: Deep core (TVA), anti-extension
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 10 each side
- **Notes**: Lower back stays flush to floor. Slow and controlled. Excellent yoga and climbing carryover.

### Plank (Standard or RKC)
- **Muscles**: Full core, shoulders
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 30–45 seconds
- **Notes**: RKC plank: squeeze glutes, pull elbows toward feet. Much harder than standard.

### Side Plank
- **Muscles**: Obliques, hip abductors
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 30s each side
- **Notes**: Can add hip dip for extra work.

### Cable Woodchop (High to Low)
- **Muscles**: Obliques, core rotation
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12 each side
- **Notes**: Rotate from torso, not just arms. Translates well to bouldering reach moves.

### Pallof Press
- **Muscles**: Anti-rotation core
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 10–12 each side
- **Notes**: Stand sideways to cable. Press and hold. One of the best core stability exercises.

### Hanging Knee/Leg Raise
- **Muscles**: Lower abs, hip flexors, grip
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 12–15
- **Notes**: Controlled swing. Straight legs for more challenge. Grip strength transfers to bouldering.

### Ab Rollout (Wheel or Barbell)
- **Muscles**: Full core, lats
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 8–12
- **Notes**: Start from knees. Don't let lower back sag.

### Russian Twist
- **Muscles**: Obliques
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15 each side
- **Notes**: Hold dumbbell or plate. Feet up for more challenge.

### Bicycle Crunch
- **Muscles**: Obliques, upper abs
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 20 each side
- **Notes**: Slow and controlled beats fast momentum. Elbow to opposite knee.

### Cable Crunch (Kneeling)
- **Muscles**: Upper abs
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15
- **Notes**: Hinge at waist — crunch, don't pull with arms.

### L-Sit Hold (Parallel Bars or Dip Station)
- **Muscles**: Core, hip flexors, triceps
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 15–20 seconds
- **Notes**: Excellent for bouldering — directly trains compression body tension.

### Hollow Body Hold
- **Muscles**: Full anterior core
- **Sets/Reps**: 3 × 20–30 seconds
- **Notes**: Lower back pressed to floor, arms overhead, legs low. The yoga/climbing athlete's core exercise.

## SCALING GUIDE

| Exercise | Clint (~205 lbs, muscular) | Wife (~110 lbs, lean/endurance) |
|---|---|---|
| DB Bench Press | 70–90 lbs total | 20–30 lbs total |
| Shoulder Press | 40–50 lbs total | 15–20 lbs total |
| Lateral Raise | 20–25 lbs | 5–8 lbs |
| Lat Pulldown | 100–130 lbs | 40–60 lbs |
| Goblet Squat | 50–60 lbs | 20–30 lbs |
| RDL | 80–100 lbs | 30–40 lbs |
| KB Swing | 53 lbs | 26–35 lbs |
| Cable Woodchop | 30–40 lbs | 10–15 lbs |

> **General Rule**: Find a weight where reps 13–15 feel genuinely challenging. Increase when you can complete all reps with 2+ reps in reserve.
